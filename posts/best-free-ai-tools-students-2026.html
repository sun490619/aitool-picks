1. NotebookLM — Best for Research Notes

NotebookLM turns PDFs and web pages into audio summaries and study guides. Upload your textbook chapters and get a podcast-style summary you can listen to while commuting.

2. Quizlet AI — Best for Flashcards

Quizlet's AI features auto-generate flashcards from your notes. The "Magic Notes" feature converts a messy outline into clean Q&A cards in seconds.

3. GitHub Copilot (Free) — Best for CS Students

GitHub Copilot is now free for all students via the GitHub Student Developer Pack. Real-time code suggestions in VS Code, plus Copilot Chat for debugging.

4. Perplexity AI — Best for Research Papers

Perplexity combines AI search with source citations. Great for literature reviews — you get answers with clickable references, not just a wall of text.

5. Grammarly (Free) — Best for Essay Writing

Grammarly's free tier catches grammar mistakes and suggests clarity improvements. Good enough for most student essays; upgrade only if you need advanced style checks.

6. Notion (Free) — Best for Organizing Notes

Notion's free plan is generous for students. Build a personal wiki, track assignments, and collaborate on group projects — all in one workspace.

7. Canva (Free) — Best for Presentations

Canva's AI Magic Design turns a text prompt into a slide deck. Good for quick class presentations where design isn't the main focus.

8. Wolfram Alpha (Free tier) — Best for STEM

Wolfram Alpha computes math, plots graphs, and solves physics problems step-by-step. The free tier handles most homework tasks.
