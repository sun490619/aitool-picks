1. ChatGPT (Free) — Best for Proposals & Outreach

ChatGPT's free tier handles most proposal writing, follow-up emails, and client communication. Use it to draft scope documents and reply to RFPs faster.

2. Canva (Free) — Best for Proposals & Social

Canva's free plan includes AI Magic Design for quick graphics. Freelancers use it for LinkedIn banners, proposal covers, and Instagram content.

3. Notion (Free) — Best for Client Management

Notion's free tier is enough for a solo freelancer. Build a client CRM, track project deadlines, and store contracts in one database.

4. Grammarly (Free) — Best for Client-Facing Writing

Grammarly catches mistakes in proposals, contracts, and client emails before you hit send. The free tier covers grammar and tone.

5. Wave (Free) — Best for Invoicing

Wave is a free invoicing and accounting tool for freelancers. Send professional invoices, track expenses, and connect your bank account — no monthly fee.

6. Calendly (Free) — Best for Scheduling

Calendly's free plan lets clients book meetings without back-and-forth emails. Set your availability, share a link, and let them pick a time.

7. Trello (Free) — Best for Project Tracking

Trello's free boards keep freelance projects organized. Use it to track deliverables, deadlines, and client feedback in one visual workflow.
