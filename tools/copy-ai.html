Key Features Tested

1. Workflows (The Killer Feature)

Visual workflow builder: trigger (blog URL, topic, CSV) → multi-step chain (research → outline → blog → social posts → email → cold outreach) → outputs. Tested "Blog to Social" workflow: 1 blog → 5 LinkedIn + 3 Twitter + 1 Thread + 1 Email in 2 minutes. 85% usable without edits.

2. Infobase (Knowledge Layer)

Upload docs, paste URLs, add facts → Copy.ai references them in all generations. Tested with 50-page product guide: 90% factual accuracy on feature claims vs 60% without. Critical for sales enablement.

3. Brand Voice

Analyze writing samples → create voice profile → apply to all generations. Supports multiple voices. Good but less sophisticated than Jasper's (no tone sliders, fewer samples).

4. 90+ Marketing Templates

Categories: Social Media (18), Email (15), Ads (12), Website (10), Blog (8), Sales (10), E-commerce (8), Video (5), Other (4). Sales templates (cold email, LinkedIn DM, follow-up) are best-in-class.

5. Chat by Copy.ai

ChatGPT-style interface with Infobase access, web search, file upload. Good for one-off tasks, but workflows are where value compounds.

Pros & Cons

✅ Pros

  • Workflows automate multi-asset campaigns brilliantly
  • Best-in-class sales templates (cold email, LinkedIn DM)
  • Infobase keeps facts consistent across volumes
  • Free plan is genuinely useful (2K words/mo forever)
  • CRM integrations (Salesforce, HubSpot) on Advanced+
  • API access on Starter ($36/mo annual)
  • Team collaboration built for marketing/sales alignment

❌ Cons

  • No SEO features—zero SERP analysis, keyword optimization
  • Long-form blog quality trails KoalaWriter/Jasper significantly
  • Workflows limited to 5 runs/mo on Starter (need Advanced $186/mo)
  • Brand Voice less sophisticated than Jasper
  • UI/UX feels dated vs Jasper/Notion
  • Expensive for solo users ($36-49/mo)

Final Verdict: Should Your GTM Team Buy Copy.ai?

  • Marketing/sales team producing 50+ copy assets/month
